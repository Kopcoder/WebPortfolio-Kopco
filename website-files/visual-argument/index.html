If You Wouldn't Smoke A Sneeze..." Image

Description

This visual argument serves as a light hearted satirical take on the anti-vaccination movement. It serves to hyperbolize the arguments into the realm of absurdity using a (hopefully) fictional argument about vaccines being as simple as injecting a sneeze directly into the body. It serves to express a misinformed voice in a humorous light.

A struggle that I had with developing and composing the rhetoric of this visual argument was defining a hard line of absurdity. Many genuine arguments against vaccination border the realms of absurdity, so clearly defining my piece as satirical became a difficult question of "Just what is unquestionable absurdity within this context?" I used very clashing visuals such as clashing colors, glaring font choices, and just all around immature tone in an attempt to achieve that absurdity, but it still didn't seem to be quite obvious enough. I determined through the help of classroom critique that framing the piece as a stance of a fictional group on Facebook would assist in defining this as absurdity. I created a fictional Facebook group called: "Brainy Mommas Against Vaccination and Also Children" to be the supposed source of this fictional argument and placed that name in a call to action at the bottom of the argument. With a name that implies that the group of mothers are against children, hopefully the absurdity of the rest of the piece and its satirical intent become apparent.

In the realm of technological struggles, there were many learning experiences while using GIMP. I used most of the different selection tools available in the program while working on the piece, I used the cropping tools to keep the images at a concise size and/or remove the background to make it transparent and flow with the rest of the piece more gracefully, I dealt with the layers with duplication and organization to help build the argument, and I used text tools alongside the rest of that (primarily the duplicate and merge layers features) to achieve the drop shadow effect behind the text. I used the flip tool to direct the faces in the images toward the center to draw the audience's attention inward. I also used the gradient effect for the background and installed an external arrow macro in order to draw the green arrows consistently, because GIMP does not have an arrow shape built in by default.
